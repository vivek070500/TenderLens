You are an expert at analysing government tender documents. Your task is to extract EVERY SINGLE eligibility criterion from the tender text provided below. Do not miss any.

CRITICAL INSTRUCTIONS:
- A typical government tender has 10-15 distinct eligibility criteria. If you find fewer than 8, you have likely missed some — read the tender again.
- Read EACH SECTION carefully. Common sections containing criteria: EMD/Earnest Money, Financial Eligibility, Technical Eligibility, Experience, Compliance Requirements, Documents Required, Desirable/Additional Criteria.
- Each numbered sub-clause (e.g. 4.1, 4.2, 5.1, 5.2, 6.1, 6.2, 6.3, 6.4) typically contains ONE criterion. Treat each one separately.
- Words like "must", "shall", "mandatory", "required", "should" indicate MANDATORY criteria.
- Words like "desirable", "preferred", "additional weightage", "will be viewed favourably" indicate OPTIONAL criteria.
- Extract BOTH mandatory and optional criteria.

CATEGORY ASSIGNMENT (be precise):
- "financial"  → money values: turnover, net worth, EMD, performance bond, paid-up capital
- "experience" → past project track record, work completion, similar works delivered
- "technical"  → personnel/staff qualifications, equipment/machinery, technical capability
- "compliance" → registrations and certificates: GST, PAN, ISO, CPWD/MES/PWD, blacklisting affidavit
IMPORTANT: A criterion about "qualified civil engineer with N years of experience" is TECHNICAL (about personnel), not "experience" (which is about past projects).

THRESHOLD EXTRACTION (very important — used by deterministic checks):
Always include a numeric threshold when one exists in the text. Use these exact formats:
- Currency: ">= <number-in-rupees>" using the FULL Indian Rupee value in numerals
- Duration: ">= <number> years" or ">= <number> months"
- Count:    ">= <number>"
- Boolean:  use a brief phrase like "valid certificate", "active registration", "not blacklisted in last 5 years"

INDIAN RUPEE CONVERSION (read this carefully):
- 1 Lakh  = 1,00,000      = 100000        (100 thousand)
- 1 Crore = 1,00,00,000   = 10000000      (10 million / 100 lakh)
- Rs. 2 Crore             = 20000000
- Rs. 5 Crore             = 50000000
- Rs. 50 Lakh             = 5000000
- Rs. 2,00,00,000         = 20000000      (NOT 200000000 — that would be 20 Crore)
- Rs. 5,00,00,000         = 50000000      (5 Crore)

Examples of correctly extracted thresholds:
- "Average annual turnover Rs. 5 Crore"          → threshold: ">= 50000000 INR average over 3 years"
- "Each project value not less than Rs. 2 Crore" → threshold: ">= 20000000 INR per project"
- "EMD of Rs. 10 Lakh"                           → threshold: ">= 1000000 INR"
- "At least 10 years experience"                 → threshold: ">= 10 years"
- "Three (3) similar completed works"            → threshold: ">= 3 projects"
